# Immigration Policy, Labor Force, and GDP (1980–2022)
**Author:** Danish Khan | **Advisor:** Dr. Linda Dynan (NKU)

## 📌 Research Summary
This study investigates how the strictness of U.S. immigration policy—proxied by the **Ratio of Deportations to Legal Immigrants (R/L)**—impacts Labor Force Participation Rates (LFPR) and Gross Domestic Product (GDP).

## 🛠 Methodology
- **Data Source:** FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data) & Dept. of Homeland Security.
- **Proxy Variable:** R/L Ratio (Deportations / Legal Immigrants).
- **Models:** - **Model 1 (LFPR):** OLS Regression with squared terms for R/L and Time to account for non-linear trends.
  - **Model 2 (GDP):** Multivariate regression analyzing the direct impact of enforcement on economic output.

## 📊 Key Results
- **Model Fit:** The GDP model showed an outstanding fit with an **Adjusted R-squared of 99.19%**.
- **GDP Impact:** A 1 percentage point increase in the R/L ratio (stricter enforcement) is associated with a **$4,389 billion decrease in GDP**, holding other factors constant.
- **LFPR Impact:** While a negative correlation exists, the R/L ratio was not a statistically significant predictor of LFPR, suggesting other factors like Wage Growth and Time are stronger drivers.
